Understanding Brick Veneer: What You Need to Know

Making informed decisions about your property starts with understanding how brick veneer works, how it differs from other construction methods, and what to expect from your installation.

Benefits of Brick Veneer Installation

Brick veneer offers homeowners and commercial property owners significant advantages that make it one of the most popular exterior finish choices:

Durability & Longevity

  • 50-100+ year lifespan with proper maintenance
  • Excellent resistance to harsh weather, including freeze-thaw cycles
  • Won't rot, warp, or deteriorate like wood siding
  • Resists insect damage and mold growth

Low Maintenance Requirements

  • No painting or staining required
  • Minimal cleaning needs (occasional washing)
  • Infrequent repointing (every 20-30 years typically)
  • Color never fades or requires refreshing

Energy Efficiency

  • Provides thermal mass that moderates temperature swings
  • Air cavity allows for full-thickness modern insulation
  • Reduces heating and cooling costs
  • Blocks wind infiltration better than many siding materials

Fire Resistance

  • Brick is a non-combustible material
  • Provides superior fire protection compared to vinyl or wood siding
  • May qualify for insurance discounts in some areas
  • Creates a fire barrier between exterior and structural walls

Weather Protection

  • Excellent resistance to rain, snow, and wind
  • Protects underlying structure from moisture damage
  • Cavity wall design allows water drainage
  • Resists impact from hail and debris

Common Brick Veneer Problems

While brick veneer is exceptionally durable, certain issues can develop over time—especially in climates with significant freeze-thaw cycles, heavy precipitation, or foundation movement. Recognizing these problems early allows for timely repairs that prevent more extensive damage:

Cracked Bricks

  • Causes: Foundation settlement, thermal expansion/contraction, impact damage, or excessive moisture
  • Signs: Visible cracks running through individual bricks, often in a stair-step pattern
  • Consequences: Water infiltration, accelerated deterioration, structural concerns if widespread
  • Solution: Professional assessment to determine cause, crack repair or brick replacement as needed

Spalling Brick

  • Causes: Water absorption followed by freeze-thaw cycles causing surface layer to flake off
  • Signs: Brick surfaces peeling, flaking, or popping off; rough, deteriorated appearance
  • Consequences: Progressive damage that worsens each winter; structural weakening
  • Solution: Replace severely spalled bricks; improve drainage to prevent moisture accumulation

Deteriorating Mortar Joints

  • Causes: Age, weathering, incorrect mortar mix, or excessive moisture exposure
  • Signs: Crumbling mortar, gaps in joints, mortar easily scraped away with a tool
  • Consequences: Water infiltration behind brick veneer; loose bricks; potential wall failure
  • Solution: Professional tuckpointing to remove deteriorated mortar and install fresh mortar

Efflorescence (White Staining)

  • Causes: Water-soluble salts migrating through brick and crystallizing on surface
  • Signs: White, powdery deposits on brick surface; often appears after rain
  • Consequences: Primarily aesthetic but indicates water infiltration issues
  • Solution: Clean deposits; identify and fix water source; improve drainage

Bulging or Bowing Walls

  • Causes: Failed wall ties, structural movement, excessive moisture, or freeze-thaw pressure
  • Signs: Visible outward movement of brick veneer; gaps between brick and structure
  • Consequences: Serious safety hazard; risk of collapse if not addressed
  • Solution: Emergency professional assessment; structural repairs; wall tie replacement

Water Stains and Dampness

  • Causes: Failed flashing, clogged weep holes, deteriorated mortar, or poor drainage
  • Signs: Dark staining on brick; water marks on interior walls behind brick
  • Consequences: Interior water damage; mold growth; structural deterioration
  • Solution: Identify water entry point; repair flashing; clear drainage; seal as needed

Gaps Between Brick and Structure

  • Causes: Foundation settlement, structural movement, or failed wall ties
  • Signs: Visible separation between brick veneer and building; cracks at corners
  • Consequences: Loss of structural connection; increased risk of wall failure
  • Solution: Professional structural assessment; wall tie repair or replacement

When to Call a Professional: If you notice any of these warning signs, contact Tibben Masonry for a thorough assessment. Early detection and professional repair prevent minor issues from becoming major structural problems that compromise your home's safety and value.
